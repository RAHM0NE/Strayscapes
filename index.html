Mapping stray lives across urban space

A common codependency overlooked.

Strayscapes is not only a tool for observing the movements and behaviors of urban stray animals — it also serves as a platform for exploring how non-human life adapts to and shapes city environments. By mapping sightings, identifying patterns of territory, and correlating animal activity with human infrastructure, Strayscapes provides valuable insights for urban planning, environmental research, and community engagement. Its applications span from informing local authorities and animal welfare groups to contributing to discussions in urban ecology, spatial justice, and more-than-human design thinking. Ultimately, Strayscapes helps us rethink how cities function when we view them through the presence of other species.
